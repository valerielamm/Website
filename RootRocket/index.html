ROOT ROCKET

ABOUT: This is a special mix of nutrients that have been horticulturalist tested and proven to help young plants in the transition from their nursery containers into the ground or a more permanent container. It ultimately helps give fresh nutrients to old soil.

MISSION: Root Rocket is an effective product that could use more marketing, a revamped logo, and branding development.

COLORS: Natural and astrological hues with various darknesses.

LOGO PROCESS

The goal was to combine nature and space in a simplistic manner. It's rendered as a seal to take advantage of the correlation between a sealâ€™s naturally circular characteristics and the shape of the earth.

 

A vine in the shape of a ring, like the ring of Saturn, is wrapped around the planet with leafs growing from it. This is used to envoke the idea of this brand's products being good for the planet.

PACKAGING

The fertilizer's packaging should reflect that it's hand-made. A paper bag would enhance the feeling of it being more personal as well as it being good for the environment.

Given that Root Rocket is sold in small quantities and that it has moisture, the fertilizer itself would be in small, portioned ziplocks inside of the paper bag.

 

Using the plastic bag and label type from their pre-existing packaging, a cleaner, more inviting design was produced.

A new fertilizer for them to provide is a personalized mix. Customers would be able to request personilized mixes to fit thier soil needs.

ADVERTISEMENT

The goal of the poster is to combine the idea of space with the idea of plants growing quickly. A plant is placed inside of an astronaut helmet, to depict it being ready to take off.

The logo is placed at the bottom right with a website for the viewer to go to. The words 'prepare for takeoff' were chosen to further re-emphasize that when this fertilizer is used, it makes plants take off fast like an astronaut in a spaceship.

WEBSITE

The website is clean and intuitive. A page is added that links to local organizations, charities, gardening, and 'how it's made' videos with a filter to enhance the hand-made, community aspects of the brand.

Fertilizer can't be shipped, so orders are able to be placed before heading to the store.
